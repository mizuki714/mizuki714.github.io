Tours

Passion Island By Land And Ferry

The most economical option to get to this fantastic all-inclusive getaway on a white sand island for an unforgettable day away from the usual tourist traps.

Meet downtown and board the vehicle for the ground transfer to the north of Cozumel, which takes about 30-45 minutes in each direction and includes a bumpy part along unpaved road through protected areas.

When you arrive you'll board the small ferry at the fisherman's dock and cross the lagoon to Passion Island. The ferry crossing takes about 5-10 minutes in each directon.

Once you arrive to the Isla Pasión beach club at your paradise island destination you can take a stroll along the white sand, go shopping, get a massage, take a swim, paddle a kayak, or simply sip a cold beer or rum punch.

Freshen up with a drink on the shoreline, while relaxing in a beach hammock or under a shady palapa hut roof.

Lunch and open bar is included, so don't forget to taste the famous Cozumel Passion Island guacamole and fajitas!

While you relax and enjoy the stunning white sand beach the kids can have fun with the water hammocks and children's games area. Free kayaks are also included for everyone to enjoy and snorkel gear, water bikes and paddle boards are also available for a small extra cost.

You'll have just over 3½ hours on the island in total before taking the ferry back with more great photo opps and then your taxi will be taking to transfer you back to the meetng point.

If you're visiting on a cruise ship and your ship docks downtown the meeting point is at your terminal. If you dock at one of the other cruise terminals you'll need to take a taxi to the meeting point, it takes about 5 minutes.

Private Snorkel boat tour

If you prefer snorkeling in a small group, away from the splashing fins and flailing arms of other snorkelers, then our private snorkel boat tour is perfect for you.

We use a fast 4 or 6 passenger boat for these private trips, guaranteeing the best personalized service.

You also have the flexibility to start at any time between 8am and 2pm, which is great for cruise passengers who are trying to fit a snorkel tour with their port times.

You'll be taken to the south-west of the island to top snorkel locations such as El Cielo*, Palancar, Colombia, Cedral and Cardona. The small boat means you can change location easily to avoid reefs that are crowded with other boats and snorkel away from the crowds.

See magnificent reef formations, colorful tropical fish and Cozumel's other forms of exciting marine life. If you're lucky you might even see a ray, turtle or clawless lobster.

The total tour time is about 4 hours and includes all snorkel equipment incuding life-vest, snorkel guide in the water with you, soft drinks, purified water, fruit snack and, after snorkeling, beers.

If you're looking for the most relaxed and personalized Cozumel snorkel experience, this is the tour to book.

Island Features

Zona Arqueológica San Gervasio

San Gervasio is an archaeological site of the pre-Columbian Maya civilization, located in the northern third of the island of Cozumel off the northeastern coast of the Yucatán Peninsula, in what is now the Mexican state of Quintana Roo. San Gervasio's pre-Hispanic name was Tantun Cuzamil, Mayan for Flat Rock in the place of the Swallows. The ruins were once a hub of worship of the goddess Ix Chel, an aged deity of childbirth, fertility, medicine, and weaving. Pre-Columbian Maya women would try to travel to San Gervasio and make offerings at least once in their lives. In 1560, the Spanish historian, Diego Lopez de Cogolludo, wrote: "The pilgrims arrive at Cozumel for the fulfillment of their vows to offer their sacrifices, to ask help for their needs, and for the mistaken adoration of their false gods." The bishop of Yucatán, Diego de Landa, wrote in 1549 that the Maya "held Cozumel in the same veneration as we have for pilgrimages to Jerusalem and Rome, and so they used to go to visit and offer presents there, as we do to holy places; and if they did not go themselves, they always sent their offerings." All-Inclusive Mayan Beach Vow Renewal

Nothing could be more romantic than a Mayan vow renewal ceremony, followed by the chance to relax together on the beach with all-inclusive food and drink?

You'll start the day by declaring your continuing love for each other, in a unique, ancient ceremony led by a shaman on the beach club's soft white sands.

flowers and professional photography are included, as the turquoise sea laps at the shore and you gaze into the eyes of your loved one.

You can customize the Mayan ceremony to include your own vows and exchange of rings if you wish, and you also have the option to include the mixing of unity sand, for a highly symbolic, romantic moment.

After the ceremony the rest of the day is yours to enjoy, ordering food and drink from the all-inclusive menu and making romatic memories.

You can enjoy the experience alone or bring along guests too for an additional fee.

This package is a truly magical, romantic way to celebrate your relationship and have many happy recollections from your time in Cozumel.

About Cozumel

Cozumel is Mexico's third-largest island, and its most populated, floating just above the Caribbean sea and measuring 28 miles long and 11 miles wide. The northern tip of the island is covered in thick coastal vegetation, and the rest of the island is accessible via one main ring road that traces the coastline of the island's lower half. The eastern side of the island is remote, with a handful of beach clubs, bars, and other fun stops.

Additional places to visit

Punta Morena: A rainbow-themed beach club with hammocks, a bar, and a pool.

Coconuts: At the top of this hill is another bar and restaurant (called Coconuts Bar & Grill) with great views.

El Pescador Beach Club: A beach club with lots of space for beach volleyball and hanging out, with tons of lounge chairs to choose from.

Palancar Beach: a gorgeous sand beach and restaurant. TIP: if you arrive before 2:30 pm, try to get on a boat to head out to the sand bar.

Turquoise Beach Bar: This restaurant is a bright blue, chic locale with a rooftop feel, since it overlooks the ocean from up high. While you wait for your food or before you find a table, head down to the water, walk down the dock, and jump in. And, there are a couple of partially submerged hammocks that you can hang out in before getting out of the water.
